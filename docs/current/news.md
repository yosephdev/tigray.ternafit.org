# Tigray News & Updates

    The humanitarian situation in Tigray is **severe and worsening** as of April 2026. Renewed armed clashes, a collapsing health system, and record famine conditions have reversed years of post-ceasefire progress. This page documents verified developments from international sources.

## April 2026

### TPLF Central Committee Votes to Reinstate Pre-War Council — Raising Conflict Risk

*April 15–16, 2026 — Axum, Tigray*

The TPLF Central Committee convened in Axum on April 15–16, 2026 and passed resolutions to reinstate the pre-war 2020 Regional Council — a direct challenge to the federally-backed Tigray Interim Regional Administration (IRA). The committee described the 2020-elected council as the "expression of our people's sovereign authority." Subsequent meetings on April 17–18 saw the "Mekete" leadership council endorse the resolutions.

The move represents an open rupture between TPLF's old guard — led by Chairman Debretsion Gebremichael — and the current IRA administration under Lt. Gen. Tadesse Worede, whose mandate was unilaterally extended by Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed on April 8. Lt. Gen. Worede warned on April 21 that the TPLF push "nullifies the Pretoria Agreement" and could lead to "renewed conflict" within Tigray.

Analysts at the ACLED Conflict Data Project describe this as potentially "cyclical escalation" echoing the political dynamics that preceded the November 2020 outbreak of full-scale war.

**Sources**: [Addis Standard](https://addisstandard.com/tigray-interim-president-says-tplf-move-to-reinstate-pre-war-council-nullifies-pretoria-agreement/) · [Martin Plaut](https://martinplaut.com/2026/04/19/tplf-statement-on-rejects-the-extended-interim-administration-of-tigray-and-calls-for-new-assembly-translation/) · [The Reporter Ethiopia](https://www.thereporterethiopia.com/50292/) · [ACLED](https://acleddata.com/expert-comment/jalale-getachew-birru-new-wave-political-instability-within-tigray)

### UN Analysis: 350,000+ in Tigray at IPC Phase 5 Famine Level

*April 2026*

A joint analysis by UN agencies and international aid organizations confirmed that more than **350,000 people in Tigray** are suffering from IPC Phase 5 "Catastrophe" conditions — the most severe level on the famine classification scale, characterized by extreme food shortage, acute malnutrition, and daily deaths. This is the highest concentration of Phase 5 conditions anywhere in the world as of this reporting period and the worst food crisis in the region in a decade.

Researchers have separately verified **1,329 hunger deaths** documented across just 9 subdistricts and 53 IDP camps over a two-week period, with starvation accounting for 49.3% of deaths in researched districts. Child malnutrition is at "alarming levels," with acute malnutrition rampant among children under five.

The broader picture: **5.4 million people** — nearly 91% of Tigray's population — require urgent humanitarian assistance (UN/OCHA).

**Sources**: [Euractiv](https://www.euractiv.com/news/more-than-350000-people-in-ethiopias-tigray-in-famine-un-analysis/) · [AP News](https://apnews.com/article/ethiopia-tigray-hunger-deaths-aid-6d5a15fa7cda213b86f41c87b1a185e7) · [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/02/1166930) · [African Security Analysis](https://www.africansecurityanalysis.org/reports/ongoing-humanitarian-crisis-in-tigray-conflict-starvation-and-displacement)

## March 2026

### Omna Tigray Quarterly Report: Food Aid Cut by More Than Half

*March 2026 — Omna Tigray Quarterly Situation Report*

The March 2026 Quarterly Situation Report from Omna Tigray documented a catastrophic decline in food assistance: **food aid beneficiaries dropped from 1.66 million to 726,000** between January and November 2025 — a reduction of more than 50%. The report warned of an imminent health-system collapse, noting that over 90% of health facilities are damaged and two-thirds are non-functional.

Aid operations face compound constraints: funding cuts, fuel restrictions, and administrative barriers are reducing humanitarian movement and supply delivery across the region.

**Source**: [Omna Tigray March 2026 Quarterly Situation Report](https://omnatigray.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/March-2026-Quarterly-Situation-Report.pdf)

## February 2026

### UN and Le Monde: Renewed Displacement as Conflict Risk Rises

*February 2026*

The UN warned in February that renewed clashes between Ethiopian federal forces (ENDF) and Tigrayan forces (TDF) are making the humanitarian situation more precarious, with civilians again being displaced. Le Monde reported that displacement camps in Mekelle, Shire, and Adigrat are already overwhelmed and unable to absorb new arrivals.

The Rift Valley Institute (RVI) published a comprehensive assessment concluding that the Pretoria Agreement has reached its "most acute point of strain" — with implementation of key provisions stalled or actively reversed, and the fundamental dispute over Western Tigray (still occupied by Amhara regional forces and Eritrean Defense Forces) unresolved.

**Sources**: [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/02/1166930) · [Le Monde](https://www.lemonde.fr/en/le-monde-africa/article/2026/02/25/tigray-in-northern-ethiopia-is-facing-the-threat-of-another-war_6750852_124.html) · [Rift Valley Institute](https://riftvalley.net/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/RVI-20260326-Tigray-CTA-PRF-Final.pdf) · [ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/ethiopia/peace-and-instability-tigray-pretoria-agreement)

### Health Infrastructure: 80% of Health Posts Damaged or Destroyed

*February 2026 reporting*

Reports from early 2026 confirm the scale of health infrastructure collapse: **80% of health posts, 73% of health centers, and all specialized hospitals in Tigray** have been damaged or destroyed. Despite commitments under the Pretoria Agreement to restore basic services, many medical facilities remain non-functional due to lack of supplies, unpaid healthcare workers, and ongoing insecurity.

Specific health challenges documented in 2026:

- **Preventable deaths** from cholera, measles, and malaria due to non-functional health facilities
- **Sexual violence legacy**: thousands of women and girls continue to suffer physical and psychological effects of systematic sexual violence committed during the war, with little access to medical or psychological support
- **IDP camp conditions**: overcrowded camps lack safe drinking water and sanitation, fueling disease outbreaks

**Source**: [African Security Analysis](https://www.africansecurityanalysis.org/reports/ongoing-humanitarian-crisis-in-tigray-conflict-starvation-and-displacement) · [Omna Tigray](https://omnatigray.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/March-2026-Quarterly-Situation-Report.pdf)

## January 2026

### TDF Operations in Tselemt; Federal Drone Strikes Follow

*January 26–30, 2026*

In late January 2026, Tigray Defense Forces (TDF) launched a major operation to seize control of the disputed Tselemt area in Western Tigray — currently claimed by the Amhara region. Intense fighting with federal troops occurred between January 26 and 29. TDF units simultaneously moved into Alamata and Korem in Southern Tigray following the withdrawal of federal command posts.

The federal government responded with **multiple drone strikes in Central Tigray** on January 30, 2026, targeting what the ENDF described as military assets. Local reports indicated the strikes hit vehicles carrying civilian goods. These clashes represent the **most significant breach of the Pretoria Agreement's ceasefire** since its signing in November 2022.

A rival armed faction — the "Tigray Peace Force" (TPF), composed of former TDF members with reported tacit federal backing — also engaged in direct combat with TDF units near the Afar border in late January, signaling the emergence of a multi-party conflict within Tigray itself.

**Sources**: [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/1/29/clashes-between-government-troops-and-tigrayan-forces-erupt-in-ethiopia) · [BBC](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cwygrnjz9wro) · [ACLED Africa Overview March 2026](https://acleddata.com/update/africa-overview-march-2026) · [Martin Plaut](https://martinplaut.com/2026/01/29/eepa-situation-report-horn-of-africa-no-699-29-january-2026/)

### Al Jazeera: 80% of Tigray's Population Needs Emergency Assistance

*January 23, 2026*

Al Jazeera reported that nearly **80 percent of the population in Tigray** is in urgent need of emergency assistance, and that aid cuts — including the suspension of USAID and WFP support following a 2023 aid theft scandal — have left many communities with very little support. While some aid has since resumed, it is frequently directed toward other global conflict hotspots, leaving Tigray with critical funding gaps.

## Background: The Pretoria Agreement and Its Erosion

The **Agreement for Lasting Peace through a Permanent Cessation of Hostilities** (CoHA) — known as the Pretoria Agreement — was signed on November 2, 2022, ending the Tigray War. Key provisions have stalled or been reversed:

| Provision | Status (April 2026) |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Cessation of hostilities | **Breached** — renewed clashes January 2026 |
| TDF disarmament | Partially completed; TDF reconstituting |
| Withdrawal of Eritrean forces | Partial — EDF remains in border areas |
| Withdrawal of Amhara forces from Western Tigray | **Not implemented** — area remains under Amhara administration |
| Return of IDPs to Western Tigray | **Blocked** — hundreds of thousands cannot return |
| Accountability for war crimes | **Not initiated** |
| Restoration of federal services | Slow, uneven |

The TPLF leadership has formally declared the agreement "completely annulled" by the actions of Addis Ababa.
