# Digital Library

This curated library provides annotated sources forming the scholarly foundation for this knowledge base.

---

## Essential Books

### Understanding Ethiopia's Tigray War

**Authors**: Martin Plaut and Sarah Vaughan  
**Publisher**: Hurst Publishers, 2023  
**ISBN**: 978-1-78738-565-4

A comprehensive account of the 2020-2022 conflict, providing historical analysis of the war's origins, the evolution of Tigrayan identity, competing visions of the Ethiopian state, and the role of Eritrea.

---

### Peasant Revolution in Ethiopia: The Tigray People's Liberation Front, 1975â€“1991

**Author**: John Young  
**Publisher**: Cambridge University Press, 1997  
**ISBN**: 978-0-521-02606-2

Foundational text on the rise of the TPLF, based on interviews with peasants and TPLF leaders. Provides essential background for understanding post-1991 political developments.

---

### Fire from the Ashes: A Chronicle of the Revolution in Tigray

**Author**: Jenny Hammond  
**Publisher**: The Red Sea Press, 1999  
**ISBN**: 978-1-56902-095-0

Journalistic chronicle of the TPLF's struggle against the Derg regime, offering on-the-ground perspectives.

---

### Foundations of an African Civilisation: Aksum and the Northern Horn

**Author**: David W. Phillipson  
**Publisher**: Boydell & Brewer, 2012  
**ISBN**: 978-1-84701-041-4

Authoritative archaeological and historical account of the Aksumite kingdom. Essential for understanding Tigray's ancient history.

## Critical Reports

### Human Rights Documentation

#### Physicians for Human Rights

**Report**: "You Will Never Be Able to Give Birth"  
**Date**: 2025  
**Topic**: Conflict-related sexual and reproductive violence

Medical-legal documentation of sexual violence during the war, providing evidence of systematic use of sexual violence as a weapon of war.

[PHR Official Report](https://phr.org/our-work/resources/you-will-never-be-able-to-give-birth/)

---

#### Amnesty International

**Report**: "Ethiopia: Massacre of hundreds of civilians in Axum"  
**Date**: February 2021

Investigation of the November 2020 Axum massacre, documenting killings by Eritrean forces.

---

#### Human Rights Watch

Multiple reports (2021-2023) documenting human rights violations during the conflict.

## Using This Library

### For Academic Research

1. Start with foundational texts for historical context
2. Consult investigation reports for documented violations
3. Cross-reference multiple sources
4. Note publication dates and source types

### For Journalism

1. Focus on investigation reports from credible organizations
2. Reference official documents and statements
3. Seek multiple source verification

### For Policy Work

1. Utilize think tank analyses
2. Reference official documentation
3. Monitor ongoing developments

## Citation Guidelines

When citing sources from this library:

- **Books**: Author, Title, Publisher, Year
- **Reports**: Organization, "Title," Date, URL
- **Articles**: Author, "Title," Journal, Volume/Issue, Year
