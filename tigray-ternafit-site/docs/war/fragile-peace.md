# Post-Conflict Situation

---

## The Cessation of Hostilities Agreement

On **November 2, 2022**, the Government of Ethiopia and the TPLF signed a Cessation of Hostilities Agreement in Pretoria, South Africa, mediated by the African Union.

### Key Provisions

The agreement included provisions for:

- Permanent cessation of hostilities
- Disarmament and demobilization processes
- Restoration of federal authority in Tigray
- Restoration of services (banking, telecommunications, transport)
- Humanitarian access
- Transitional justice mechanisms

---

## Implementation Status

*Note: Implementation is ongoing and assessments vary. The following reflects reported developments.*

### Progress Reported

- Formal end to active military operations
- Gradual restoration of some services
- Resumption of some humanitarian access
- Political dialogue processes initiated

### Ongoing Challenges

International observers and analysts have noted:

- Incomplete implementation of agreement provisions
- Continued presence of non-signatory forces in some areas
- Delayed restoration of full services
- Ongoing humanitarian needs
- Questions about accountability implementation

---

## Territorial Issues

### Western Tigray

The status of western Tigray remains a significant issue:

- Areas under Amhara regional administration since November 2020
- Displaced populations unable to return
- Territorial disputes unresolved by peace agreement
- Eritrea not a party to the agreement

### Eritrean Forces

- Eritrea was not a signatory to the Pretoria Agreement
- Reports of continued Eritrean military presence in some areas
- Withdrawal provisions of agreement applying only to Ethiopian forces
