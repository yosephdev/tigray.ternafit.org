# Human Rights Documentation

!!! danger "Content Warning"
    This section contains documentation of war crimes, mass killings, sexual violence, and other serious human rights violations. Content is based on investigations by international organizations. Reader discretion is advised.

---

## Overview

International investigations have documented widespread and systematic violations of international humanitarian law during the Tigray War. Multiple parties to the conflict have been implicated in serious violations.

**Attribution Note**: The findings presented below are attributed to the investigating organizations cited. This platform does not independently verify individual incidents.

---

## International Investigations

Documentation is based primarily on investigations by:

- **UN International Commission of Human Rights Experts on Ethiopia (ICHREE)**
- **Amnesty International**
- **Human Rights Watch**
- **Physicians for Human Rights**
- **Joint Ethiopian Human Rights Commission (EHRC) and UN OHCHR investigation**

---

## Documented Violations

### Mass Killings

International investigations documented multiple mass killing incidents:

#### Axum (November 2020)

- **Investigating Organization**: Amnesty International
- **Finding**: Documented killings of hundreds of civilians
- **Alleged Perpetrators**: Eritrean forces identified
- **Source**: Amnesty International report, February 2021

#### Mai Kadra (November 2020)

- **Investigating Organization**: Ethiopian Human Rights Commission, international reporting
- **Finding**: Mass killings documented
- **Context**: Disputed attribution; different parties accused
- **Note**: This incident remains subject to differing accounts

#### Other Documented Sites

International organizations have documented killings at multiple additional locations throughout the conflict. For specific documentation, consult the original investigation reports.

---

### Sexual and Gender-Based Violence

Multiple investigations documented widespread sexual violence during the conflict.

#### Key Findings (Attributed to Investigating Organizations)

**Physicians for Human Rights** documented:

- Systematic use of sexual violence
- Evidence of reproductive harm
- Findings consistent with sexual violence as a weapon of war
- *Source*: PHR report "You Will Never Be Able to Give Birth" (2025)

**UN Commission of Human Rights Experts** concluded:

- Sexual violence used as a weapon of war
- Violations constituting crimes against humanity
- All parties to the conflict implicated

**Amnesty International** documented:

- Widespread rape and sexual violence
- Systematic targeting
- Multiple perpetrator groups identified

---

### Forced Displacement

#### Western Tigray

International organizations documented:

- Large-scale displacement of Tigrayan population
- Transfer of territorial control
- Ongoing displacement situation

#### Internal Displacement

- Estimates of over 2 million internally displaced at peak
- Multiple waves of displacement throughout conflict
- *Note*: Displacement figures are estimates and vary across sources

---

### Humanitarian Access Restrictions

International organizations documented:

- Restrictions on humanitarian aid delivery
- Disruption of essential services
- Banking and communications restrictions
- *Investigating Organizations*: UN OCHA, humanitarian agencies

---

### Cultural Heritage

Documentation of damage to cultural and religious sites:

- Reports of looting and damage to churches and monasteries
- Theft of religious artifacts reported
- Historic sites affected
- *Note*: Full assessment pending due to access limitations

---

## Legal Classification

International investigations have characterized documented violations as:

- **War crimes** under international humanitarian law
- **Crimes against humanity** under the Rome Statute
- Potential **genocide** indicators identified by some analysts (disputed)

**Note**: Legal determinations are made by competent judicial bodies. Classifications above reflect findings of investigating organizations.

---

## Accountability

### International Mechanisms

- UN Human Rights Council established investigation commission
- Various countries opened investigations under universal jurisdiction
- Targeted sanctions imposed by some governments

### Pretoria Agreement Provisions

The November 2022 peace agreement includes provisions for:

- Transitional justice mechanisms
- Accountability processes
- *Note*: Implementation is ongoing

---

## Limitations of Documentation

- Access restrictions limited investigation scope
- Communications blackout hampered real-time documentation
- Some areas remain inaccessible for verification
- Full scope of violations may not be documented
