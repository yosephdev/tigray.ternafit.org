# Humanitarian Crisis

!!! warning "Content Advisory"
    This section documents humanitarian emergency conditions including famine, healthcare collapse, and mass displacement.

---

## Overview

The Tigray War precipitated a severe humanitarian crisis affecting millions of people. International humanitarian organizations documented widespread food insecurity, healthcare system collapse, and mass displacement.

**Note**: Statistics in this section are estimates from international organizations. Precise figures remain difficult to verify due to access restrictions during the conflict.

---

## Food Insecurity

### Scale

International organizations reported:

- Millions of people requiring emergency food assistance at peak crisis
- Severe acute malnutrition documented in affected populations
- Agricultural disruption affecting food production capacity

*Sources*: UN OCHA, WFP situation reports

### Contributing Factors

Documented factors included:

- **Agricultural destruction**: Reports of crop burning and livestock killing
- **Market disruption**: Destruction of local markets and trade networks
- **Access restrictions**: Limitations on humanitarian aid delivery
- **Economic disruption**: Banking services disruption affecting aid operations

### Aid Delivery Challenges

International organizations reported significant obstacles to humanitarian response:

- Restrictions on convoy movements
- Fuel shortages affecting distribution
- Bureaucratic obstacles to access
- Security concerns for humanitarian personnel

---

## Healthcare System

### Infrastructure Impact

International assessments indicated:

- Significant damage to health facilities
- Looting of medical equipment reported
- Disruption of medical supply chains
- Healthcare worker displacement

*Note*: Specific percentages of facilities affected vary across assessments and time periods.

### Public Health Consequences

Reported impacts included:

- Disruption of vaccination programs
- Limited access to maternal and child health services
- Mental health needs among affected populations
- Increased disease risk due to displacement conditions

---

## Displacement

### Internal Displacement

- Estimates of over 2 million internally displaced persons at peak
- Multiple waves of displacement as conflict areas shifted
- Displacement from rural areas to urban centers
- Use of schools, churches, and public buildings as shelters

*Sources*: IOM, UNHCR estimates

### Refugee Outflows

- Tens of thousands of refugees documented in Sudan
- Camps established in Kassala and Gedaref states
- Challenging conditions in refugee settings reported

### Conditions

Documented challenges for displaced populations:

- Inadequate shelter
- Limited access to clean water and sanitation
- Food insecurity
- Protection concerns, particularly for women and children

---

## Education

### Disruption

- Schools closed throughout conflict period
- School buildings used for military purposes or as shelters
- Educational materials destroyed in some areas
- Years of educational disruption for affected children

### Impact on Students

- Significant numbers of children out of school
- Learning loss documented
- Concerns about long-term educational impacts
- Increased risk of school dropout

---

## Water and Sanitation

Documented impacts:

- Damage to water infrastructure
- Disruption of urban water systems
- Increased reliance on unsafe water sources
- Sanitation challenges in displacement settings

---

## International Response

### UN and International Agencies

- UN agencies maintained humanitarian operations despite access challenges
- Funding appeals issued for humanitarian response
- Coordination challenges documented

### Access Issues

International organizations consistently reported:

- Significant restrictions on humanitarian access
- Delays in aid delivery
- Challenges reaching affected populations

---

## Recovery Needs

Post-conflict assessments have identified needs including:

- Continued food assistance during recovery
- Healthcare system reconstruction
- Educational infrastructure rehabilitation
- Water and sanitation system repair
- Psychosocial support for affected populations
- Support for displaced populations and returnees

---

## Data Limitations

- Access restrictions limited assessment capacity
- Figures represent estimates with uncertainty
- Conditions varied significantly across regions and time periods
- Full impact assessment ongoing
